01 Financial performance

Revenue rose from RMB 50.1m in 2023 to RMB 103m in 2024 and RMB 337m in 2025, a 159.5% compound annual growth rate. The company turned profitable in 2025 with net profit attributable to shareholders of RMB 28.7m, versus losses of RMB 25.9m in 2023 and RMB 13.3m in 2024; net profit excluding non-recurring items was RMB 15.1m. Gross margin expanded from 33.5% in 2023 to 52.8% in 2025. Frost & Sullivan ranked DeepRobotics first globally in industrial quadruped robot application revenue and second in overall quadruped robot revenue in 2025.

Valuation: Implied post-money from the offering: RMB 2.503bn raise for no less than 18% of post-IPO share capital. Pre-IPO Series C valued the company near RMB 8bn (~US$1.1bn) in Dec 2025.

02 Core product lines
Jueying X30
Flagship industrial quadruped for inspection, rated for extreme temperatures from -20C to 55C.
Jueying Lite3
Lower-cost quadruped aimed at education, research, and developer use.
Lynx (M20)
Wheeled-legged hybrid quadruped with interchangeable wheels and feet for mixed terrain.
DR02
All-weather bipedal humanoid targeting industrial manipulation, successor to the DR01 prototype.
Jueying X20
Industrial-grade waterproof quadruped for inspection and rescue missions.
03 Key history
2017
Founded in Hangzhou by Zhu Qiuguo and Li Chao; first Jueying quadruped launched.
2021-08
Launched Jueying X20 industrial waterproof quadruped for inspection and rescue.
2023-10
Launched Jueying X30, rated for -20C to 55C operation.
2024-08
Unveiled DR01 humanoid at the World Robot Conference.
2025-12
Closed Series C of over RMB 500m (~US$71m); began IPO tutoring.
2026-05
STAR Market IPO application accepted; seeking to raise RMB 2.503bn.

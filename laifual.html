01 Financial performance

Revenue rose 142% to RMB261m in 2025 from RMB108m in 2024, and RMB94.5m in 2023, driven by harmonic reducer shipments doubling to 292,000 units. The company remains loss-making, with net losses near RMB169m to RMB171m across 2023 to 2025, though adjusted net loss narrowed to RMB8.9m in 2025. Gross margin was 25.6% in 2025, down from 29.5% in 2023. Average selling price fell from roughly RMB802 in 2023 to RMB573 in 2025 as the company prioritised volume over margin.

Valuation: Day-one closing market capitalisation; shares fell 14.6% from the HK$85.50 IPO price to HK$73, with the IPO raising about US$147m.

02 Core product lines
FS / LS Series harmonic reducers
Cup-type flexspline harmonic reducers for robotic arms, automation and precision machinery.
LH Series harmonic reducers
Hat-type reducers with a large through-hole mounting surface for compact joint designs.
Joint modules
T-type integrated joint modules with zero-backlash harmonic gearing for cobots and humanoids.
Robotic arms
6-axis collaborative robotic arms built on in-house reducers and joint modules.
Automation workstations
Turnkey automated workstations integrating Laifual motion components.
03 Key history
2013
Company founded in Shengzhou, Zhejiang, co-established by local bearing and academic partners.
2015
Zhang Jie assumes operational control, shifting strategy from high-margin niche to volume penetration.
2016
Enters the harmonic reducer market; mass production begins in 2017.
2022
Series D round led by the National Manufacturing Transformation and Upgrade Fund, with Lenovo Capital co-investing.
2026-06

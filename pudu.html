01 Financial performance

Absolute revenue is not publicly disclosed. Management reported revenue roughly doubling (about 100% growth) in 2025, with commercial cleaning now over 70% of revenue. The company says EBITDA is near breakeven, positioning it among the closest to profitability in commercial service robotics. Its last private round was ~US$150m in April 2026 at a US$1.5bn valuation.

Valuation: Post-money from ~US$150m (near RMB 1bn) Series round closed April 2026; no IPO price set.

02 Core product lines
Commercial cleaning
CC1 and CC1 Pro four-in-one autonomous scrubbers plus SH1 upright scrubber; over 70% of revenue.
Service delivery
BellaBot and KettyBot lines for restaurants, hotels and retail; the original core business.
Industrial delivery
T-series autonomous mobile robots (AMRs) including T150 for light-payload factory and warehouse logistics.
Embodied AI / humanoid
D9 full-size bipedal humanoid and D7 industrial semi-humanoid built on the PuduFM 1.0 foundation model.
03 Key history
2016
Founded in Shenzhen by Felix Zhang Tao, initially targeting restaurant delivery robots.
2024-12
Unveiled PUDU D9, its first full-size bipedal humanoid robot.
2025-05
Launched AI-powered CC1 Pro cleaning robot; cleaning segment scaled past 70% of revenue.
2025
Revenue roughly doubled year over year; cumulative shipments passed 120,000 units.
2026-04
Raised ~US$150m (near RMB 1bn) at a US$1.5bn valuation; investors include BAIC, Meituan, Shenzhen Investment Holdings.
2026-06
Founder confirmed preparations for a Hong Kong (or mainland) IPO; cumulative shipments passed 130,000 units.

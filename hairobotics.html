01 Financial performance

Revenue reached RMB 1.36bn in 2024, up from RMB 807m in 2023, then RMB 1.26bn in the first nine months of 2025 (up 36% YoY). Gross margin improved to 28.9% in 2024 from 16% in 2023, with overseas gross margin above 40%. The company remains loss-making; the 9M 2025 net loss of RMB 589m narrowed about 40% YoY. Total liabilities stood at roughly RMB 3.88bn as of September 2025, and operating cash flow was still negative.

Valuation: Last reported private valuation from a 2021 growth round; no IPO price set. Company filed with HKEX in Feb 2026; deal size not yet disclosed.

02 Core product lines
HaiPick ACR (A42 series)
Flagship autonomous case-handling robots that retrieve totes and cartons from racks up to 10m high.
HaiPick Climb (HaiClimber)
Compact high-density goods-to-person system delivering up to 4,000 totes per hour from ~1,000 sqm.
HaiPort
Automated loading and unloading workstations that pair with the robot fleet.
HaiFlex
Mixed-fleet AGV solution scheduled alongside HaiPick robots.
HAI Q software
Warehouse orchestration platform that schedules over 1,000 robots across HaiPick, HaiPort and HaiFlex.
03 Key history
2015
Founder Richie Chen develops the first autonomous case-handling robot (ACR), the HaiPick.
2016
Hai Robotics founded in Shenzhen to commercialise the ACR category.
2020
Closes US$100m Series B financing to scale R&D and international expansion.
2021
Raises further growth capital reported at a US$1.3bn valuation; expands overseas footprint.
2024
Becomes world's largest ACR provider with over 30% market share by revenue and shipments; revenue hits RMB 1.36bn.
2025-08
Appoints Adrian Stoch, former GXO Chief Automation Officer, as CEO Americas.
2026-02
Files for a Hong Kong Main Board IPO.

01 Financial performance

Agility remains unprofitable and pre-scale on revenue. Operating expenses rose to about US$111m in 2025 from roughly US$71m in 2024, with cash burn near US$100m for the year. It reports more than US$300m of multi-year contracted Digit v5 orders as backlog, and has raised about US$683m in total funding to date including a US$400m Series C in 2025.

Valuation: Pre-money equity value in the announced Churchill Capital Corp XI merger; deal expected to deliver more than US$620m gross proceeds, including a roughly US$200m PIPE at US$10 per share led by Foxconn.

02 Revenue & cost drivers
What drives revenue
Digit v5 contracted backlog
More than US$300m of multi-year Digit v5 orders reported as backlog at deal announcement.
Robotics-as-a-Service model
RaaS subscription converts hardware capex into recurring opex for logistics customers, building sticky revenue.
Warehouse deployments
Pilots and deployments at GXO and Amazon anchor early commercial revenue; scale not yet disclosed.
What weighs on cost
Operating expense growth
Opex rose to about US$111m in 2025 from roughly US$71m in 2024, per preliminary unaudited SPAC filings.
Cash burn
Company burned roughly US$100m of cash in 2025 while pre-scale on revenue.
Manufacturing and R&D scale-up
RoboFab production ramp toward a 10,000-unit milestone drives capex and headcount cost.
Total capital deployed
About US$683m raised to date funds losses ahead of humanoid unit economics being proven.
03 Core product lines
Digit v5
Bipedal warehouse humanoid; lifts up to 50 lb, ~22 hours per charge, reaches 7.2 ft.
Agility Arc
Cloud platform for deploying, coordinating and managing fleets of Digit robots.
NVIDIA Halos safety stack
Digit v5 is launch partner for NVIDIA Halos, using IGX Thor and Halos Core for functional safety.

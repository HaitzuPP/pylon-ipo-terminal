01 Financial performance

Revenue was RMB 54.0m in 2023, RMB 55.5m in 2024, then RMB 258m in 2025, a three-year CAGR of about 119%. The 2025 jump was driven by full-size Kuavo units, which contributed RMB 178m, or 68.9% of revenue. The company remains loss-making: net losses of RMB 41.1m (2023), RMB 60.1m (2024) and RMB 71.2m (2025). Management guides to profitability no earlier than 2028.

Valuation: Implied by the RMB 2.6bn IPO raise target; last private round (pre-IPO, Sept 2025) put post-money at RMB 4.327bn.

03 Core product lines
Kuavo (夸父)
Full-size humanoid; runs Huawei-linked HarmonyOS (KaihongOS) and pairs with Huawei Pangu models.
Aelos
Small educational biped; featured in the Beijing 8-minute segment at the 2018 Winter Olympics.
Roban (鲁班)
Small-to-mid education robot line aimed at schools and training markets.
04 Key history
2016-03
Founded in Shenzhen by HIT PhDs Leng Xiaokun, Chang Lin and An Ziwei; launches Aelos biped.
2018
Aelos robots feature in the Beijing 8-minute performance at the PyeongChang Winter Olympics.
2023-11
Launches Kuavo, described as the first humanoid built on Huawei open-source HarmonyOS (KaihongOS).
2024-03
Huawei Cloud partnership to combine Pangu models with the Kuavo humanoid.

Begins mass production of Kuavo humanoids.

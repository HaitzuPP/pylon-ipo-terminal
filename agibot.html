01 Financial performance

AgiBot does not publicly disclose revenue; the business is pre-mass-commercialisation. The company reported shipping more than 5,100 humanoid units in 2025, which it and third-party trackers put near 39% of global humanoid shipments. Its last private round valued AgiBot above RMB 15bn (about US$2.1bn) in March 2025. The A-share shell it is taking over, Swancor Advanced Materials (688585), posted FY2024 revenue of RMB 1.49bn (up 6.7%) and net profit of RMB 88.7m (up 25%).

Valuation: Reuters-reported HK IPO valuation target for a Q3 2026 listing; last private round put AgiBot above RMB 15bn (about US$2.1bn) in March 2025.

02 Core product lines
Yuanzheng (Expedition) series
Full-size bipedal humanoids for industrial, interactive and research use, e.g. the 1.75m A2.
Lingxi (Genie) series
Compact, agile humanoids such as the GO-1-powered X2 (about 1.3m, 31 DoF), with open-source variants.
GO-1 (Genie Operator-1)
Generalist embodied foundation model that learns tasks from human video; lifted task success from 46% to 78%.
AgiBot World
Open-source large-scale robot manipulation dataset and learning platform for embodied AI training.
03 Key history
2023-02
Founded in Shanghai by ex-Huawei executives Deng Taihua and Peng Zhihui.
2023-08
Unveiled first Yuanzheng (Expedition) humanoid robots.
2025-03
Launched GO-1 embodied foundation model and Lingxi X2; valuation passed RMB 15bn.
2025-07
Agreed to acquire at least 63.62% of STAR-listed Swancor (688585) for about RMB 2.1bn.
2025-10
Reuters reports a planned Hong Kong IPO targeting HK$40-50bn (US$5.1-6.4bn).
2025-11
Converted to a joint-stock company and renamed Zhiyuan Innovation, a pre-IPO step.
2026
Targeting a Hong Kong listing by Q3 2026, led by CICC, CITIC Securities and Morgan Stanley.

01 Financial performance

FY2025 revenue reached RMB 724m, up 132% year on year, after RMB 124m in 2023 and RMB 312m in 2024. The FY2025 net loss widened to about RMB 4.72bn on R&D spending of roughly RMB 3.18bn. On-premise model deployments to state-owned enterprises, banks and government bodies produced roughly 85% of revenue, leaving a heavy cash-burn profile. The January 2026 HKEX IPO raised about HK$4.35bn (US$558m); a July 2026 placement raised a further HK$31.4bn (US$4.0bn).

Valuation: Market cap per Wind data on HKEX-listed 2513 (share price ~HK$2,078), converted at HKD/USD ~7.8; up from ~HK$57.5bn on the 8 Jan 2026 debut.

02 Revenue & cost drivers
What drives revenue
On-premise deployment84.5%
MaaS / API and services15.5%
FY2024 split per HKEX prospectus; on-premise to SOEs, banks and government stayed roughly 85% into 2025.
What weighs on cost
R&D and compute spend
FY2025 R&D roughly RMB 3.18bn (RMB 2.2bn in 2024) is the main loss driver.
Revenue-mix shift to on-premise
Lower-margin deployment revenue cut gross margin from 64.6% (2023) to 41% (2025).
Domestic model price competition
API price war with DeepSeek, Moonshot and MiniMax pressures unit economics.
03 Core product lines
GLM foundation models
Flagship LLM family (GLM-4.5, GLM-4.6, GLM-5) sold via API and licensing.
Model-as-a-Service platform
bigmodel.cn and Z.ai developer platform serving GLM models over API.
On-premise enterprise deployment
Private model installs for SOEs, banks and government; roughly 85% of revenue.
AutoGLM and AI agents
Autonomous agents for task automation and embodied-AI applications.
Multimodal models
CogVideoX video, CogView image and GLM-4V vision models.
04 Key history
2019
Spun out of Tsinghua University's KEG lab; founded by Tang Jie, Zhang Peng and Liu Debing.
2022
Released GLM-130B open bilingual foundation model.
2023
Launched ChatGLM; raised large rounds from Alibaba, Tencent and others at roughly US$2.8bn valuation.
2024
Adopted the Z.ai international brand and shipped the GLM-4 model family.
2025-04
Pre-filed for a Hong Kong IPO.
2026-01
Listed on HKEX at HK$116.20, the world's first pure-play LLM company to go public.
2026-07
Priced a HK$31.4bn (US$4.0bn) share placement at HK$1,588; market cap near HK$814bn.
